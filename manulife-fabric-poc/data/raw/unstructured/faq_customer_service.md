## General Account Questions

### How do I access my Manulife account online?

You can access your account through the Manulife online portal at manulife.ca/signin or through the Manulife mobile app, available for iOS and Android. To register for the first time, you will need your policy or contract number and your date of birth. If you experience difficulty logging in, you can reset your password online or call our Customer Service Centre at 1-888-626-8543.

### How do I update my personal information (address, phone number, email)?

You can update your contact information through any of the following channels:
- **Online**: Log in at manulife.ca/signin and navigate to Profile > Personal Information
- **Mobile App**: Tap Menu > My Profile > Edit
- **Phone**: Call 1-888-626-8543 (Monday to Friday, 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM ET)
- **Mail**: Send a signed written request to Manulife, P.O. Box 1602, Waterloo, ON N2J 4C6

Please note that certain changes, such as a legal name change, require supporting documentation (e.g., government-issued photo ID, marriage certificate, or legal name change certificate).

### How do I obtain a copy of my policy?

You can request a copy of your policy by calling 1-888-626-8543 or by submitting a written request. A duplicate policy will be mailed to your address on file within 10 business days. Digital copies are available for policies issued after January 2020 through the online portal.

## Claims

### How do I file a life insurance claim?

To file a life insurance claim, follow these steps:
1. Call our Claims Department at 1-888-626-8543 and select the claims option. A claims representative will guide you through the process and send you the required forms.
2. Complete the Claimant's Statement form and gather the required documents, including a certified copy of the death certificate and proof of your identity.
3. Submit the completed forms and documents online at manulife.ca/claims, by mail, or through your financial advisor.
4. Our claims team will review your submission and contact you if additional information is needed.
5. Once approved, payment is typically processed within 5 business days.

### How do I file a health or dental claim?

For group health and dental claims:
- **Fastest option**: Use the Manulife Plan Member app to photograph and submit your receipts directly from your smartphone. Most claims are processed within 2 business days.
- **Online**: Log in at groupbenefits.manulife.ca and submit your claim electronically.
- **Mail**: Complete a Group Benefits Claim Form and mail it with original receipts to Manulife, P.O. Box 670, Waterloo, ON N2J 4B8.

Claims must be submitted within 12 months of the date the expense was incurred. Your Explanation of Benefits (EOB) will be available online within 48 hours of processing.

### How long does it take to process a claim?

Processing times vary by claim type:
- **Group health and dental claims**: 2-5 business days for electronic submissions; 10-15 business days for paper submissions
- **Life insurance death claims**: 15-30 business days from receipt of all required documentation
- **Critical illness claims**: 15-30 business days from receipt of all required documentation
- **Disability claims**: 20-30 business days for the initial assessment

If your claim requires additional investigation or documentation, processing may take longer. You will be notified of any delays.

### What if my claim is denied?

If your claim is denied, you will receive a written explanation of the reasons for the denial. You have the right to appeal the decision. To appeal:
1. Review the denial letter carefully to understand the specific reason(s).
2. Gather any additional documentation that may support your claim.
3. Submit a written appeal to the Claims Manager within 90 days of the denial.
4. If your appeal is unsuccessful, you may contact the OmbudService for Life & Health Insurance (OLHI) at 1-888-295-8112 or olhi.ca for an independent review.

## Beneficiaries

### How do I change my beneficiary?

To change your beneficiary:
1. Download the Change of Beneficiary form from manulife.ca/forms or request one by calling 1-888-626-8543.
2. Complete the form, including the full legal name, date of birth, and relationship of each new beneficiary, along with the percentage allocation.
3. Sign and date the form. Your signature must match the signature on file.
4. Submit the form by mail or through your financial advisor.

Please note: If your current beneficiary is designated as irrevocable, you cannot change the designation without the written consent of the irrevocable beneficiary. In Quebec, a beneficiary designated as a spouse is irrevocable by default under the Civil Code of Quebec.

### Can I name multiple beneficiaries?

Yes, you may name multiple primary beneficiaries and specify the percentage each should receive (e.g., 50% to Spouse, 25% each to two children). You may also name contingent beneficiaries who would receive the benefit if no primary beneficiary survives the insured person. The total allocation for primary beneficiaries must equal 100%.

### What happens if I don't name a beneficiary?

If no beneficiary is designated, or if all designated beneficiaries predecease the insured person, the death benefit will be paid to the estate of the insured person. Payment to the estate may be subject to probate fees and may be accessible to the estate's creditors. We strongly recommend keeping your beneficiary designations current.

## Premium Payments

### What payment options are available for my insurance premiums?

Manulife offers the following premium payment methods:
- **Pre-authorized debit (PAD)**: Monthly, quarterly, semi-annual, or annual debits from your Canadian bank account. This is the most convenient option and may qualify for a premium discount.
- **Credit card**: Visa, Mastercard, or American Express. Monthly payments only.
- **Online banking**: Set up Manulife as a payee through your bank's online bill payment service. Use your policy number as the account number.
- **Cheque or money order**: Payable to "Manulife" and mailed with your payment stub.

### Can I change my payment frequency?

Yes, you can change your payment frequency (e.g., from monthly to annual) by calling 1-888-626-8543 or by submitting a request through the online portal. Changes take effect on the next premium due date. Note that annual payment may result in a lower total premium compared to monthly payments due to the annual payment discount.

### What happens if I miss a premium payment?

If a premium payment is missed, your policy enters a 31-day grace period. During the grace period, your coverage remains in force. If payment is not received by the end of the grace period, your policy will lapse. To reinstate a lapsed policy, you may need to provide evidence of insurability and pay all overdue premiums with interest. Contact us as soon as possible if you are having difficulty making payments so that we can discuss available options.

## Policy Cancellation

### How do I cancel my insurance policy?

To cancel your policy, you must submit a written request. You may:
- Call 1-888-626-8543 to request a cancellation form
- Download the form at manulife.ca/forms
- Send a signed letter to Manulife, P.O. Box 1602, Waterloo, ON N2J 4C6

Before cancelling, please consider speaking with your financial advisor about alternatives, such as reducing coverage, converting to a paid-up policy, or taking advantage of policy loan provisions. Cancelling a life insurance policy means you may not be able to obtain similar coverage in the future if your health has changed.

### Will I receive any money back if I cancel?

- **Term life insurance**: If cancelled within the first 10 days (free-look period), you will receive a full refund of premiums paid. After the free-look period, no refund is provided.
- **Whole life and universal life insurance**: You may receive the Cash Surrender Value (CSV), if any, less any outstanding policy loans. The CSV depends on how long the policy has been in force and the specific policy terms. Note that surrendering a policy with a gain may have tax consequences.

## Investment Accounts

### How do I access my Manulife investment account?

Log in at manulife.ca/investments or use the Manulife mobile app. You can view your account balances, transaction history, fund performance, and statements. If you have a group retirement account (Group RRSP, DPSP, or pension), access it at retireonline.manulife.com.

### How do I make a contribution to my RRSP or TFSA?

You can make contributions through:
- **Online**: Log in and select Make a Contribution. You can contribute via electronic funds transfer from your bank account.
- **Pre-authorized contribution (PAC)**: Set up automatic recurring contributions on a weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly basis.
- **Lump sum**: Mail a cheque payable to "Manulife" with your account number noted.
- **Through your advisor**: Your financial advisor can process contributions on your behalf.

Please ensure your contributions do not exceed your available RRSP or TFSA contribution room to avoid penalties from the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).

### How do I switch between investment funds?

You can switch between funds within your account at no charge (subject to the short-term trading fee for units held less than 30 days). To make a switch:
- **Online**: Log in and select Switch Funds. Follow the prompts to select the source and destination funds.
- **Phone**: Call 1-888-588-7999 to speak with an investment representative.
- **Through your advisor**: Your financial advisor can submit a switch request on your behalf.

Switches are processed at the net asset value calculated at the end of the business day on which the request is received (if before 4:00 PM ET).

## Tax and Regulatory Questions

### Will I receive tax slips from Manulife?

Yes. Manulife issues the following tax slips as applicable:
- **T4A**: Pension and annuity income, RRSP/RRIF withdrawals
- **T5**: Investment income (interest, dividends) from non-registered accounts
- **T4RSP**: RRSP withdrawals
- **T4RIF**: RRIF withdrawals
- **RRSP contribution receipts**: For RRSP contributions made during the tax year

Tax slips are available online by the end of February each year and are mailed by the CRA deadline.

### Is my life insurance benefit taxable?

In most cases, life insurance death benefits paid to a named beneficiary are received tax-free in Canada. However, certain situations may trigger tax consequences, such as policy dispositions, transfers, or payments to the estate. Consult a qualified tax advisor for advice specific to your situation.
