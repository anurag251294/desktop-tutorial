# Manulife Group Health Insurance Policy Terms and Conditions

**Policy Form: ML-GH-2025-ON**
**Effective Date: January 1, 2025**
**Issued by: The Manufacturers Life Insurance Company (Manulife)**

---

## 1. Introduction and Scope of Coverage

This Group Health Insurance Policy provides eligible plan members and their dependants with coverage for medically necessary health care expenses not covered by provincial health insurance plans (OHIP, RAMQ, etc.). This policy is issued to the Plan Sponsor (the employer) and provides benefits to eligible employees as plan members.

Coverage under this policy is supplemental to the benefits provided under the applicable provincial health insurance plan and is subject to the terms, conditions, limitations, and exclusions described herein.

---

## 2. Eligibility and Enrollment

### 2.1 Eligible Persons

Eligible persons include:
- Active full-time employees of the Plan Sponsor who have completed the applicable waiting period (typically 3 months of continuous employment)
- Part-time employees working a minimum of 20 hours per week, if specified in the benefits booklet
- Eligible dependants, including the plan member's legal spouse or common-law partner and dependent children under age 21 (or under age 25 if enrolled as full-time students)

### 2.2 Late Enrollment

Plan members who do not enroll within 31 days of becoming eligible must provide evidence of insurability satisfactory to Manulife. Late enrollment may result in exclusions for pre-existing conditions.

### 2.3 Termination of Coverage

Coverage terminates on the earliest of:
- The date the plan member's employment ends
- The date the plan member ceases to meet eligibility requirements
- The date the group policy is terminated
- The date the plan member reaches age 70

---

## 3. Extended Health Care Benefits

### 3.1 Prescription Drug Coverage

This plan covers eligible prescription drugs that require a prescription from a licensed physician, dentist, or nurse practitioner. Coverage is subject to:

- **Formulary**: Manulife's managed drug formulary, which is based on the Canadian Drug Expert Committee (CDEC) recommendations. Generic substitution is mandatory where a generic equivalent is available.
- **Dispensing Fee Cap**: Maximum dispensing fee of $12.00 per prescription.
- **Reimbursement**: 80% of eligible expenses after the deductible, unless otherwise stated in the benefits booklet.
- **Annual Maximum**: $10,000 per plan member per calendar year for prescription drugs.
- **Specialty Drugs**: Drugs costing more than $10,000 per year require prior authorization through Manulife's Specialty Drug Program.

### 3.2 Hospital Coverage

Semi-private hospital room accommodation is covered at 100% of the hospital's standard semi-private differential, for medically necessary hospital stays within Canada.

### 3.3 Paramedical Services

Coverage for licensed paramedical practitioners, subject to the following per-practitioner annual maximums:
- Physiotherapist: $1,500 per year
- Psychologist: $2,000 per year
- Chiropractor: $750 per year
- Massage Therapist: $750 per year
- Naturopath: $500 per year
- Osteopath: $500 per year
- Speech Therapist: $1,500 per year
- Dietitian/Nutritionist: $500 per year

A physician referral is required for psychologist and speech therapist services.

### 3.4 Vision Care

- Eye examinations: one exam every 24 months, up to $100
- Corrective lenses and frames or contact lenses: $300 every 24 months
- Laser eye surgery: $500 per eye, lifetime maximum

### 3.5 Medical Equipment and Supplies

Coverage for medically necessary equipment and supplies, including:
- Prosthetic devices (limbs, breast prostheses)
- Orthopaedic shoes and custom orthotics (up to $400 per year)
- Hearing aids ($700 every 36 months)
- Diabetic supplies
- Mobility aids (wheelchairs, walkers), subject to prior authorization

---

## 4. Deductibles and Copayments

### 4.1 Annual Deductible

An annual deductible of $50 per individual or $100 per family applies to extended health care benefits. The deductible resets on January 1 of each calendar year.

### 4.2 Copayment (Coinsurance)

After the deductible, Manulife reimburses 80% of eligible expenses. The plan member is responsible for the remaining 20% (copayment), up to the annual out-of-pocket maximum.

### 4.3 Out-of-Pocket Maximum

The annual out-of-pocket maximum is $2,000 per individual or $4,000 per family. Once the out-of-pocket maximum is reached, Manulife reimburses 100% of eligible expenses for the remainder of the calendar year.

---

## 5. Pre-Existing Conditions

### 5.1 Definition

A pre-existing condition is any illness, injury, or medical condition for which the plan member received medical consultation, diagnosis, treatment, or medication during the 12 months immediately preceding the effective date of coverage.

### 5.2 Limitation

Benefits for pre-existing conditions are excluded for the first 12 months of coverage, unless the plan member was enrolled within 31 days of initial eligibility. This limitation does not apply to plan members transferring from a prior group insurance plan with comparable coverage and no gap in coverage exceeding 90 days.

---

## 6. Network Providers

### 6.1 Preferred Provider Organization (PPO)

Manulife maintains a network of preferred providers across Canada. Plan members are encouraged to use network providers for the following advantages:
- Direct billing (no need to pay upfront and submit claims)
- Negotiated rates, reducing out-of-pocket costs
- Verified credential and quality standards

### 6.2 Out-of-Network Providers

Plan members may use out-of-network providers, but reimbursement is based on the Manulife Usual, Reasonable, and Customary (URC) fee schedule. The plan member is responsible for any amount exceeding the URC schedule.

### 6.3 Provider Directory

A current directory of network providers is available at groupbenefits.manulife.ca or by calling 1-800-268-6195.

## 7. Exclusions

The following are not covered under this policy:

1. Services or supplies covered by any provincial health insurance plan or government program
2. Cosmetic procedures, except reconstructive surgery following an accident or medically necessary mastectomy
3. Experimental or investigational treatments not approved by Health Canada
4. Services rendered outside Canada, except as covered under the Emergency Travel Assistance benefit
5. Workers' Compensation Board (WSIB/WCB) claims
6. Services provided by a family member of the plan member
7. Expenses for which no charge would be made in the absence of insurance
8. Fertility treatments, including in-vitro fertilization (IVF), unless specified in the benefits booklet
9. Weight loss programs and related medications, unless treating a diagnosed medical condition
10. Over-the-counter medications and supplements not requiring a prescription

---

## 8. Claims Submission

### 8.1 How to Submit

Claims may be submitted through:
- **Manulife Plan Member App**: Photo submission of receipts via smartphone
- **Online**: groupbenefits.manulife.ca
- **Mail**: Manulife Claims Department, P.O. Box 670, Waterloo, ON N2J 4B8

### 8.2 Time Limit

Claims must be submitted within 12 months of the date the expense was incurred. Claims submitted after 12 months will be denied.

### 8.3 Coordination of Benefits

If the plan member or dependant is covered under more than one group health plan, benefits will be coordinated in accordance with the Canadian Life and Health Insurance Association (CLHIA) Guidelines for the Coordination of Benefits. The plan of the person who incurred the expense pays first (birthday rule applies for dependent children).

---

## 9. Continuation of Coverage

### 9.1 Leave of Absence

Coverage may be continued during an approved leave of absence (including maternity/parental leave), subject to continued premium payment, for up to 24 months.

### 9.2 Conversion Privilege

Upon termination of group coverage, the plan member may convert to an individual health insurance policy within 31 days without evidence of insurability, subject to the terms and premiums of the individual plan then available.
