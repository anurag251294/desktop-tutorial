# Manulife Individual Life Insurance Policy Terms and Conditions

**Policy Form: ML-LI-2025-ON**
**Effective Date: January 1, 2025**
**Issued by: The Manufacturers Life Insurance Company (Manulife)**

---

## 1. Definitions

Throughout this policy, the following terms shall have the meanings described below:

- **Insured Person**: The individual whose life is insured under this policy, as named on the Policy Schedule.
- **Policy Owner**: The person or entity who owns this policy and has the right to exercise all ownership rights.
- **Beneficiary**: The person(s) or entity(ies) designated to receive the death benefit upon the death of the Insured Person.
- **Face Amount**: The base amount of life insurance coverage, as stated on the Policy Schedule.
- **Premium**: The amount payable to keep this policy in force, as described in the Premium Payment section.
- **Policy Date**: The date on which this policy takes effect, as shown on the Policy Schedule.
- **Policy Anniversary**: Each annual anniversary of the Policy Date.
- **Grace Period**: A 31-day period following the premium due date during which the policy remains in force.
- **Cash Surrender Value (CSV)**: The amount payable upon voluntary termination of this policy, net of any outstanding policy loans and accrued interest.
- **Contestability Period**: The first two years from the Policy Date during which Manulife may contest the validity of the policy based on material misrepresentation.

## 2. Coverage Details

### 2.1 Death Benefit

Upon receipt of satisfactory proof of death of the Insured Person while this policy is in force, Manulife will pay the death benefit to the designated Beneficiary. The death benefit is equal to the Face Amount, plus any additional amounts payable under any riders attached to this policy, less any outstanding policy loans and accrued interest, and less any overdue premiums.

### 2.2 Types of Coverage Available

This policy may be issued as one of the following:

- **Term Life Insurance (T10, T20, T30)**: Coverage for a specified term of 10, 20, or 30 years. Premiums are level for the initial term and increase upon renewal.
- **Whole Life Insurance**: Permanent coverage with level premiums payable for life or to a specified age (e.g., paid-up at age 65).
- **Universal Life Insurance**: Flexible premium permanent coverage with an investment component. The Policy Owner may adjust premiums and death benefits within the limits specified in the Policy Schedule.

### 2.3 Effective Date of Coverage

Coverage begins on the Policy Date, provided the first premium has been paid and the policy has been delivered to the Policy Owner while the Insured Person is alive and insurable as represented in the application.

## 3. Exclusions and Limitations

### 3.1 Suicide Exclusion

If the Insured Person dies by suicide, whether sane or insane, within two years from the Policy Date or from the date of any reinstatement, the liability of Manulife shall be limited to the return of premiums paid, less any outstanding policy loans and accrued interest.

### 3.2 Material Misrepresentation

During the Contestability Period, Manulife may rescind this policy if the application contained a material misrepresentation that influenced the acceptance of the risk or the terms of coverage. After the Contestability Period, this policy shall be incontestable except for non-payment of premiums or fraud.

### 3.3 War and Military Service

Death resulting directly or indirectly from declared or undeclared war, or any act of war, while the Insured Person is serving in the military forces of any country, is excluded from coverage unless a supplementary War Risk rider has been purchased.

### 3.4 Aviation Exclusion

Death resulting from the Insured Person acting as a pilot or crew member of any aircraft is excluded unless declared and accepted at the time of underwriting.

### 3.5 Hazardous Activities

This policy does not cover death resulting from participation in hazardous activities including but not limited to skydiving, hang gliding, bungee jumping, or motor racing, unless such activities were disclosed and accepted during underwriting.

## 4. Beneficiary Designations

### 4.1 Primary and Contingent Beneficiaries

The Policy Owner may designate one or more primary Beneficiaries and one or more contingent Beneficiaries. Contingent Beneficiaries receive the death benefit only if no primary Beneficiary survives the Insured Person.

### 4.2 Revocable and Irrevocable Designations

Unless designated as irrevocable, a beneficiary designation is revocable and may be changed by the Policy Owner at any time by written notice to Manulife. An irrevocable beneficiary designation cannot be changed without the written consent of the irrevocable beneficiary.

### 4.3 Changing Beneficiaries

To change a beneficiary designation, the Policy Owner must submit a completed Change of Beneficiary form to Manulife. The change takes effect on the date the form is signed, provided it is received by Manulife. Manulife is not liable for any payment made before the change is recorded.

### 4.4 Simultaneous Death

If the Insured Person and the primary Beneficiary die simultaneously or under circumstances where it cannot be determined who died first, the death benefit will be paid as if the Insured Person survived the Beneficiary, unless the policy specifies otherwise. This is consistent with the applicable provincial Survivorship Act.

### 4.5 Minor Beneficiaries

If a Beneficiary is a minor at the time the death benefit becomes payable, Manulife may pay the benefit to a trustee or legal guardian, or into court, in accordance with applicable provincial legislation.

## 5. Premium Payment Terms

### 5.1 Premium Due Dates

Premiums are due on the dates specified in the Policy Schedule. Premiums may be payable annually, semi-annually, quarterly, or monthly, as elected by the Policy Owner.

### 5.2 Premium Payment Methods

Premiums may be paid by:
- Pre-authorized debit from a Canadian bank account
- Credit card (Visa, Mastercard, or American Express)
- Direct billing with cheque or money order payable to Manulife
- Payroll deduction, where available through the Policy Owner's employer

### 5.3 Grace Period

A Grace Period of 31 days from the premium due date is allowed for the payment of each premium after the first. This policy remains in force during the Grace Period. If the Insured Person dies during the Grace Period, the overdue premium will be deducted from the death benefit.

### 5.4 Lapse and Reinstatement

If a premium is not paid by the end of the Grace Period, this policy will lapse. A lapsed policy may be reinstated within five years of the date of lapse, subject to:
- Submission of a reinstatement application
- Evidence of insurability satisfactory to Manulife
- Payment of all overdue premiums with interest at the rate specified in the Policy Schedule
- Repayment of any outstanding policy loans with accrued interest

### 5.5 Premium Waiver

If the Insured Person becomes totally disabled as defined in the Premium Waiver rider (if attached), premiums may be waived during the period of disability. See the Premium Waiver rider for complete terms.

## 6. Cash Surrender Value and Policy Loans

### 6.1 Cash Surrender Value

Whole Life and Universal Life policies accumulate a Cash Surrender Value (CSV) after the policy has been in force for a minimum period, as specified in the Policy Schedule. The CSV is calculated based on the policy reserves less any applicable surrender charges. A table of guaranteed minimum cash surrender values is included in the Policy Schedule.

### 6.2 Surrender of Policy

The Policy Owner may surrender this policy at any time by submitting a written request to Manulife. Upon surrender, Manulife will pay the CSV less any outstanding policy loans and accrued interest. Surrender terminates all coverage under this policy.

### 6.3 Policy Loans

The Policy Owner may borrow against the CSV of this policy, up to 90% of the CSV, at any time while the policy is in force. Policy loans bear interest at the rate specified in the Policy Schedule, compounded annually. Unpaid interest is added to the loan balance. If the total loan balance (including accrued interest) exceeds the CSV, this policy will terminate after 31 days' written notice to the Policy Owner.

### 6.4 Automatic Premium Loan

If elected by the Policy Owner, an Automatic Premium Loan provision allows Manulife to automatically advance a policy loan to pay any premium due, provided sufficient CSV is available. This provision prevents policy lapse due to missed premium payments.

### 6.5 Tax Implications

Surrender of a policy or withdrawal of cash value may have tax consequences under the Income Tax Act (Canada). The Policy Owner should consult a qualified tax advisor. Manulife will issue a T5 slip for any taxable policy gain as required by the Canada Revenue Agency.

## 7. Claims Procedures

### 7.1 Filing a Claim

To file a death benefit claim, the claimant must:

1. Notify Manulife as soon as reasonably possible following the death of the Insured Person by calling 1-888-626-8543 or by visiting manulife.ca/claims.
2. Complete and submit a Claimant's Statement form.
3. Provide the following documentation:
   - Certified copy of the death certificate
   - Original policy document, if available
   - Proof of identity of the claimant
   - Proof of beneficiary designation
   - If applicable, police report, coroner's report, or autopsy results

### 7.2 Proof of Claim

Satisfactory proof of claim must be provided within one year of the date of death. Manulife reserves the right to request additional information or documentation as reasonably necessary to process the claim.

### 7.3 Investigation

Manulife may investigate any claim, including but not limited to:
- Verifying the cause of death
- Confirming the identity of the Insured Person
- Reviewing medical records of the Insured Person
- Verifying the accuracy of statements made in the application

### 7.4 Payment of Claim

Upon approval of a claim, Manulife will pay the death benefit within 30 business days. Payment may be made as a lump sum or, if elected by the Beneficiary, under one of the settlement options described in the Policy Schedule.

### 7.5 Settlement Options

The Beneficiary may elect to receive the death benefit under one of the following settlement options:
- **Lump Sum**: Single payment of the full death benefit amount
- **Interest Income**: Death benefit held on deposit with interest paid periodically
- **Fixed Period**: Equal payments over a specified period
- **Life Annuity**: Periodic payments for the lifetime of the Beneficiary

### 7.6 Dispute Resolution

If a claim is denied, the claimant may appeal by submitting a written request for review to the Manulife Ombudsman. If the dispute remains unresolved, the claimant may contact the OmbudService for Life & Health Insurance (OLHI) or pursue resolution through the courts.

## 8. General Provisions

### 8.1 Entire Contract

This policy, including the application (which is attached to and forms part of this policy), the Policy Schedule, and any riders or amendments, constitutes the entire contract between the parties.

### 8.2 Incontestability

After this policy has been in force during the lifetime of the Insured Person for a period of two years from the Policy Date, it shall be incontestable except for non-payment of premiums or fraud.

### 8.3 Misstatement of Age or Sex

If the age or sex of the Insured Person has been misstated, the death benefit will be adjusted to the amount that the premiums paid would have purchased at the correct age and sex.

### 8.4 Assignment

The Policy Owner may assign this policy as collateral security. Manulife is not bound by any assignment unless it receives written notice of the assignment at its head office. Manulife is not responsible for the validity of any assignment.

### 8.5 Currency

All amounts under this policy are in Canadian dollars.

### 8.6 Governing Law

This policy is governed by the laws of the province in which the Policy Owner resides at the time of issue, and by the Insurance Companies Act (Canada) and applicable federal and provincial regulations, including guidelines issued by the Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI).

### 8.7 Privacy

Manulife collects, uses, and discloses personal information in accordance with its Privacy Policy and applicable privacy legislation, including the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA). The complete Privacy Policy is available at manulife.ca/privacy.

## 9. Rider Summaries

The following optional riders may be attached to this policy. Full terms are contained in the rider documents:

- **Accidental Death and Dismemberment (AD&D)**: Provides an additional benefit if death or dismemberment results from an accident.
- **Critical Illness**: Pays a lump sum benefit upon diagnosis of a covered critical illness.
- **Child Term Rider**: Provides term life insurance coverage for the Insured Person's children.
- **Guaranteed Insurability Rider**: Allows the Insured Person to purchase additional coverage at specified future dates without evidence of insurability.
- **Premium Waiver on Disability**: Waives premiums if the Insured Person becomes totally disabled.
