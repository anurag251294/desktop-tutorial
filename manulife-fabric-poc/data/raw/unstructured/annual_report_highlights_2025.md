# Manulife Financial Corporation Annual Report Highlights 2025

**Fiscal Year Ended December 31, 2025**
**Published: February 2026**

---

## Message from the President and CEO

2025 was a year of strong execution and strategic progress for Manulife. We delivered record core earnings, advanced our digital transformation agenda, and strengthened our position as a leading international financial services group. Our diversified business model across insurance, wealth and asset management, and banking services in Canada, the United States, and Asia proved resilient in a year marked by shifting interest rate environments and geopolitical uncertainty.

We remain focused on our ambition to be the most digital, customer-centric global company in our industry. In 2025, we made significant investments in artificial intelligence, data analytics, and digital customer experiences that are already improving client outcomes and operational efficiency.

## Strategic Priorities

### 1. Accelerate Growth in Asia

Asia remains our highest-growth market, with core earnings growing 14.3% in 2025. We expanded our agency force in Vietnam and the Philippines, launched new bancassurance partnerships in Indonesia, and grew our high-net-worth segment in Hong Kong and Singapore. Our agency force across Asia now numbers over 110,000 licensed agents. We are targeting $3.0 billion in Asian core earnings by 2027.

### 2. Strengthen Canadian Market Leadership

In Canada, we focused on deepening our position in group benefits, individual insurance, and group retirement. Key achievements include:
- Launched Manulife Vitality 2.0, an enhanced wellness program that integrates wearable device data with personalized health recommendations and premium incentives
- Won 47 new group benefits mandates representing over 125,000 new plan members
- Expanded our FHSA (First Home Savings Account) product offering with five new fund options
- Achieved record group retirement plan sales with $12.3 billion in new deposits

### 3. Grow Global Wealth and Asset Management

Our Global Wealth and Asset Management segment delivered strong results driven by market appreciation and positive net inflows. Strategic priorities include:
- Expanding our private markets platform (private credit, real estate, infrastructure, and timber/agriculture)
- Growing our institutional mandate business in North America and Asia
- Launching new ETF products in the Canadian market (four new ETFs launched in 2025)
- Continuing the shift to fee-based advisory relationships, with Series F assets now representing 38% of retail AUM (up from 31%)

### 4. Optimize U.S. Operations

In the United States, operating as John Hancock, we focused on improving profitability and growing long-term care and life insurance. The John Hancock Vitality program continued to attract health-conscious consumers, and we expanded our partnership with a leading technology provider to offer more personalized wellness experiences.

## Digital Transformation Initiatives

### AI and Data Analytics

- **Automated underwriting**: Expanded our AI-powered underwriting platform to cover 68% of individual life applications in Canada without human intervention (average decision time: 4 minutes). Straight-through processing rate increased from 42% to 68%.
- **Claims intelligence**: Deployed machine learning models to detect claims fraud, identify subrogation opportunities, and predict claims severity. These models contributed an estimated $85 million in savings in 2025.
- **Customer insights**: Implemented natural language processing (NLP) for analysis of customer communications and call centre interactions, enabling proactive identification of at-risk customers and targeted retention campaigns.
- **Investment analytics**: Deployed AI-driven portfolio optimization tools across our institutional and retail investment platforms, improving risk-adjusted returns and operational efficiency.

### Digital Customer Experience

- **Manulife Move app**: Our redesigned mobile app now provides integrated access to insurance, investments, and banking, with biometric login, real-time notifications, and AI-powered chat support. Active mobile users grew 25% in 2025.
- **E-application and e-policy delivery**: 78% of new individual life applications are now submitted digitally, and 91% of policy documents are delivered electronically, reducing paper consumption by an estimated 42 million pages.
- **Digital claims**: Our digital claims submission portal processed 2.1 million health and dental claims in 2025, with an average processing time of 3.2 days (compared to 5.1 days in 2024 and 8.4 days in 2023).

### Cloud and Infrastructure

- Completed migration of 85% of critical workloads to cloud infrastructure (Microsoft Azure and AWS), improving scalability, resilience, and cost efficiency. Target: 95% by end of 2026.
- Implemented a unified data platform using Microsoft Fabric for enterprise analytics, enabling real-time reporting and cross-functional data sharing across the organization.

## Capital Management and Shareholder Returns

Manulife's capital position remains strong, with a LICAT ratio of 137% at year-end 2025, well above the supervisory target of 100% and the internal target of 125%. We returned $5.8 billion to shareholders in 2025 through dividends ($2.8 billion) and share buybacks ($3.0 billion). The Board approved a 6.8% increase in the quarterly dividend, effective Q1 2026.

Our credit ratings remain investment grade with a stable outlook:
- AM Best: A+ (Superior)
- S&P Global: AA- (Very Strong)
- Moody's: A1 (Good)
- DBRS Morningstar: AA (low)

## Outlook for 2026

We expect continued growth in 2026, supported by:
- Favourable demographic trends driving demand for retirement, wealth management, and health insurance products
- Ongoing digital transformation reducing costs and improving customer experience
- Strong capital position enabling continued investment in growth and shareholder returns
- Expansion in Asia, our fastest-growing market

We are targeting core earnings growth of 8-10% in 2026 and a core ROE of 17%+. Our medium-term target is to achieve $10 billion in annual core earnings by 2028.
