## 1. Introduction

Manulife offers a comprehensive suite of wealth management solutions designed to help Canadians achieve their financial goals at every stage of life. Our investment products are available through registered accounts (RRSP, TFSA, RRIF, RESP, RDSP, FHSA) and non-registered accounts, and are distributed through licensed financial advisors across Canada.

This product guide provides an overview of our core fund lineup, risk characteristics, fee structures, and suitability guidelines. Advisors should use this guide in conjunction with the Fund Facts documents, simplified prospectuses, and the Manulife Suitability Assessment Framework when making investment recommendations.

All funds described herein are managed by Manulife Investment Management Limited, a wholly owned subsidiary of The Manufacturers Life Insurance Company.

## 2. Fund Lineup Overview

### 2.1 Canadian Equity Funds

**Manulife Canadian Large Cap Equity Fund (MLI-5100)**

- **Objective**: Long-term capital growth by investing primarily in equity securities of large-capitalization Canadian companies listed on the TSX.
- **Benchmark**: S&P/TSX Composite Index
- **Risk Rating**: Medium
- **Minimum Investment**: $500 initial, $50 subsequent
- **Top Holdings**: Royal Bank of Canada, Shopify Inc., Canadian Natural Resources, Brookfield Asset Management, Enbridge Inc.
- **Sector Allocation**: Financials (32%), Energy (17%), Technology (12%), Industrials (10%), Materials (9%), Other (20%)
- **Suitability**: Investors with a medium to long-term time horizon (5+ years) seeking exposure to the Canadian equity market. Suitable for growth-oriented investors with moderate risk tolerance.

**Manulife Canadian Small Cap Growth Fund (MLI-5120)**

- **Objective**: Capital appreciation by investing in small and mid-capitalization Canadian companies with high growth potential.
- **Benchmark**: S&P/TSX SmallCap Index
- **Risk Rating**: Medium-High
- **Minimum Investment**: $500 initial, $50 subsequent
- **Suitability**: Investors with a long-term time horizon (7+ years) and higher risk tolerance seeking above-average growth potential. This fund has higher volatility than large-cap equity funds and may experience significant short-term fluctuations.

### 2.2 U.S. and Global Equity Funds

**Manulife U.S. Equity Fund (MLI-5200)**

- **Objective**: Long-term capital growth by investing primarily in equity securities of large and mid-capitalization U.S. companies.
- **Benchmark**: S&P 500 Index (CAD)
- **Risk Rating**: Medium
- **Minimum Investment**: $500 initial, $50 subsequent
- **Currency**: Holdings are in USD; the fund is unhedged. Returns are affected by USD/CAD exchange rate fluctuations.
- **Suitability**: Investors seeking geographic diversification beyond Canada with exposure to the world's largest equity market. Suitable for growth-oriented investors with a 5+ year time horizon.

**Manulife Global Equity Fund (MLI-5250)**

- **Objective**: Long-term capital growth through a diversified portfolio of equity securities from developed and emerging markets worldwide.
- **Benchmark**: MSCI World Index (CAD)
- **Risk Rating**: Medium
- **Geographic Allocation**: United States (55%), Europe (20%), Asia-Pacific (15%), Emerging Markets (7%), Canada (3%)
- **Suitability**: Investors seeking broad global diversification. Core holding for a globally diversified portfolio.

### 2.3 Fixed Income Funds

**Manulife Canadian Bond Fund (MLI-5300)**

- **Objective**: Generate income and preserve capital by investing primarily in investment-grade Canadian fixed income securities, including government and corporate bonds.
- **Benchmark**: FTSE Canada Universe Bond Index
- **Risk Rating**: Low-Medium
- **Duration**: 6.5 years (as of December 31, 2024)
- **Credit Quality**: AAA (35%), AA (25%), A (25%), BBB (15%)
- **Yield to Maturity**: 4.2% (as of December 31, 2024)
- **Suitability**: Conservative investors seeking income and capital preservation. Suitable as the fixed income component of a balanced portfolio. Investors should be aware that bond prices decline when interest rates rise.

**Manulife High Yield Bond Fund (MLI-5320)**

- **Objective**: Generate above-average income by investing primarily in below-investment-grade (high yield) corporate bonds issued in North America.
- **Benchmark**: ICE BofA US High Yield Index (CAD-Hedged)
- **Risk Rating**: Medium
- **Yield to Maturity**: 6.8% (as of December 31, 2024)
- **Suitability**: Investors seeking higher income who are willing to accept greater credit risk. Not suitable as a sole fixed income holding. Best used as a complement to investment-grade bond holdings.

### 2.4 Balanced and Asset Allocation Funds

**Manulife Balanced Fund (MLI-5400)**

- **Objective**: Provide a combination of income and long-term capital growth through a diversified portfolio of Canadian and global equities and fixed income securities.
- **Benchmark**: 60% MSCI World Index / 40% FTSE Canada Universe Bond Index
- **Risk Rating**: Low-Medium
- **Target Asset Mix**: 60% equities, 40% fixed income (rebalanced quarterly)
- **Suitability**: Investors seeking a single-fund solution with moderate growth and income. Suitable for investors with a 3-5 year time horizon and moderate risk tolerance.

**Manulife Target Date Retirement Funds (MLI-5450 series)**

- **Objective**: Provide a diversified portfolio that automatically adjusts its asset allocation from growth-oriented to income-oriented as the target retirement date approaches.
- **Available Vintages**: 2030, 2035, 2040, 2045, 2050, 2055
- **Risk Rating**: Varies by vintage (Medium-High for distant dates, Low-Medium for near dates)
- **Glide Path**: Equity allocation decreases from approximately 90% (30 years before target date) to 50% (at target date) to 40% (10 years after target date).
- **Suitability**: Investors who prefer a professionally managed, hands-off approach to retirement investing. Select the vintage closest to the investor's expected retirement year.

### 2.5 Money Market Fund

**Manulife Money Market Fund (MLI-5500)**

- **Objective**: Preserve capital and maintain liquidity while generating modest income through investment in short-term Canadian money market instruments.
- **Benchmark**: FTSE Canada 91-Day T-Bill Index
- **Risk Rating**: Low
- **Holdings**: Government of Canada treasury bills, provincial government notes, high-quality commercial paper, bankers' acceptances
- **Average Term to Maturity**: 60 days
- **Current Yield**: 4.0% (as of December 31, 2024)
- **Suitability**: Investors seeking a temporary parking place for cash, or those with an extremely low risk tolerance. Not suitable for long-term wealth accumulation due to returns that may not outpace inflation.

### 2.6 Real Estate Fund

**Manulife Canadian Real Estate Investment Fund (MLI-5600)**

- **Objective**: Generate income and long-term capital appreciation through investment in a diversified portfolio of Canadian real estate investment trusts (REITs) and real estate operating companies.
- **Benchmark**: S&P/TSX Capped REIT Index
- **Risk Rating**: Medium
- **Sector Allocation**: Industrial (30%), Residential (25%), Retail (20%), Office (10%), Diversified (15%)
- **Distribution Yield**: 4.5% (as of December 31, 2024)
- **Suitability**: Investors seeking income and diversification benefits from real estate exposure. Real estate investments may be less liquid than other equity investments and are sensitive to interest rate changes.

## 3. Risk Ratings and Methodology

### 3.1 Risk Classification

Manulife classifies investment fund risk using the Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA) standardized risk classification methodology, as required by National Instrument 81-102. Risk ratings are based on the standard deviation of fund returns over the most recent 10 years (or a reference index for newer funds).

| Risk Category | Standard Deviation Range |
|---|---|
| Low | 0 to less than 6 |
| Low-Medium | 6 to less than 11 |
| Medium | 11 to less than 16 |
| Medium-High | 16 to less than 20 |
| High | 20 or greater |

### 3.2 Investor Risk Profiles

| Profile | Suitable Risk Ratings | Typical Time Horizon |
|---|---|---|
| Conservative | Low, Low-Medium | 1-3 years |
| Moderate | Low-Medium, Medium | 3-5 years |
| Balanced | Medium | 5-7 years |
| Growth | Medium, Medium-High | 7-10 years |
| Aggressive Growth | Medium-High, High | 10+ years |

## 4. Fee Structures

### 4.1 Management Expense Ratio (MER)

The MER includes the management fee, operating expenses, and applicable taxes (HST). MERs are expressed as an annual percentage of net asset value.

| Fund Category | Advisor Series (A) | Fee-Based Series (F) | Institutional Series (I) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Canadian Equity | 2.15% | 0.95% | 0.65% |
| U.S./Global Equity | 2.35% | 1.10% | 0.75% |
| Fixed Income | 1.45% | 0.65% | 0.40% |
| Balanced | 2.05% | 0.90% | 0.60% |
| Money Market | 0.85% | 0.45% | 0.20% |
| Real Estate | 2.25% | 1.05% | 0.70% |
| Target Date | 2.10% | 0.95% | 0.65% |

### 4.2 Sales Charges

- **No-Load (NL)**: No front-end or deferred sales charge. Available on all funds.
- **Front-End Load (FE)**: Negotiable between 0% and 5% of the purchase amount, paid to the advisor at the time of purchase.
- **Low-Load (LL)**: Deferred sales charge applies if redeemed within 3 years: Year 1 = 3%, Year 2 = 2%, Year 3 = 1%, thereafter = 0%.

Note: Effective June 1, 2022, deferred sales charges (DSC) are prohibited for new purchases in accordance with CSA regulations. The Low-Load option is available only for existing accounts opened prior to that date.

### 4.3 Short-Term Trading Fee

A short-term trading fee of 2% of the redemption amount applies to units redeemed within 30 days of purchase. This fee is paid to the fund to protect long-term investors from the costs of frequent trading.

### 4.4 Switch Fees

Switches between Manulife funds within the same account are generally free of charge. A maximum switch fee of 2% may apply for switches from money market funds to equity funds within 30 days.

## 5. Performance Benchmarks and Reporting

### 5.1 Performance Reporting

Fund performance is reported net of all fees (MER) and is calculated in accordance with Global Investment Performance Standards (GIPS). Performance is reported for the following periods: 1 month, 3 months, year-to-date, 1 year, 3 years, 5 years, 10 years, and since inception (all periods greater than 1 year are annualized).

### 5.2 Benchmarks

Each fund is measured against a relevant market index as its primary benchmark. Advisors should compare fund performance to the benchmark over a full market cycle (typically 3-5 years) rather than short-term periods.

### 5.3 Fund Facts

Fund Facts documents, as required by National Instrument 81-101, are provided to investors before or at the point of sale. These documents contain key information about the fund, including risk ratings, past performance, and costs. Current Fund Facts are available at manulife.ca/investments.

## 6. Regulatory Disclosures

### 6.1 Securities Regulation

Manulife's segregated funds are governed by the Insurance Companies Act (Canada) and are regulated by the Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI). Mutual funds are governed by National Instrument 81-102 and regulated by the Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA) and the applicable provincial securities commissions.

### 6.2 Guarantee Disclosure (Segregated Funds)

Segregated fund contracts provide maturity and death benefit guarantees that are not available with mutual funds. The guarantees are based on the amounts invested (less withdrawals) and are subject to conditions, including holding the investment to the maturity date (typically 10 or 15 years from deposit). Guarantees are backed by Manulife's general fund assets and are subject to Manulife's financial strength and claims-paying ability.

### 6.3 Investor Protection

- Mutual fund investors are protected by the Canadian Investor Protection Fund (CIPF) against dealer insolvency.
- Segregated fund policyholders are protected by Assuris against insurer insolvency, up to the limits described at assuris.ca.
- Deposits in Manulife Bank are insured by the Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC) up to applicable limits.

### 6.4 Know Your Client (KYC) and Suitability

Advisors must complete a KYC assessment for every client and ensure that all investment recommendations are suitable based on the client's investment objectives, risk tolerance, time horizon, and financial circumstances. Refer to the Manulife Advisor Compliance Handbook for detailed suitability assessment procedures.
