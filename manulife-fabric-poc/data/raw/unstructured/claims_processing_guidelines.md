## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document provides standardized guidelines for claims adjusters, team leads, and claims managers within Manulife's Individual Insurance Claims division. These guidelines apply to all individual life insurance, critical illness, disability, and accidental death and dismemberment (AD&D) claims processed in Canada.

All claims personnel are expected to follow these guidelines to ensure consistent, fair, and timely adjudication of claims in compliance with the Canadian Life and Health Insurance Association (CLHIA) Right to Know Guidelines, the Unfair or Deceptive Acts or Practices provisions, and Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI) supervisory expectations.

---

## 2. Claims Submission Requirements

### 2.1 Notification

Claimants should notify Manulife of a potential claim as soon as reasonably practicable. Initial notification may be made through:
- Telephone: 1-888-626-8543 (Individual Claims)
- Online: manulife.ca/claims
- Mail: Manulife Claims Department, P.O. Box 1602, Waterloo, ON N2J 4C6
- Through the claimant's financial advisor

Upon notification, the claims intake team assigns a claim number and sends the appropriate claim forms within 2 business days.

### 2.2 Required Forms

| Claim Type | Required Forms |
|---|---|
| Life Insurance Death Claim | Claimant's Statement (Form LI-CS-01), Attending Physician's Statement (Form LI-APS-01) |
| Critical Illness | Member's Statement (Form CI-MS-01), Attending Physician's Statement (Form CI-APS-01), Specialist Report |
| Disability (LTD/WOP) | Member's Statement (Form DI-MS-01), Attending Physician's Statement (Form DI-APS-01), Employer's Statement (Form DI-ES-01) |
| AD&D | Claimant's Statement (Form AD-CS-01), Accident Report, Police Report (if applicable) |

### 2.3 Proof of Claim Deadline

Claimants must submit completed claim forms and supporting documentation within the timeframe specified in the policy contract, typically:
- Life Insurance: Within 1 year of the date of death
- Critical Illness: Within 90 days of diagnosis
- Disability: Within 90 days of onset of disability
- AD&D: Within 1 year of the date of the accident

Late submissions may be accepted at the discretion of the claims manager if the claimant can demonstrate reasonable cause for the delay.

## 3. Documentation Checklist

### 3.1 Life Insurance Death Claims

The adjuster must obtain and verify the following before approving a death claim:

- [ ] Completed Claimant's Statement (signed and dated)
- [ ] Certified copy of the death certificate (provincial vital statistics)
- [ ] Attending Physician's Statement or hospital records
- [ ] Original policy document (or statutory declaration of loss)
- [ ] Government-issued photo identification of the claimant
- [ ] Proof of beneficiary status (birth certificate, marriage certificate, or court order)
- [ ] Policy application (retrieved from underwriting file)
- [ ] If death occurred within the contestability period (first 2 years): full medical records, toxicology reports, coroner's report
- [ ] If death was accidental: police report, accident reconstruction report, witness statements
- [ ] If death occurred outside Canada: consular death certificate, translated and notarized

### 3.2 Critical Illness Claims

- [ ] Completed Member's Statement
- [ ] Attending Physician's Statement confirming diagnosis
- [ ] Specialist report (e.g., oncologist for cancer, cardiologist for heart attack)
- [ ] Pathology or diagnostic imaging reports
- [ ] Survival period confirmation (30-day survival period must be met)
- [ ] Treatment records

### 3.3 Disability Claims

- [ ] Completed Member's Statement
- [ ] Attending Physician's Statement with functional limitations
- [ ] Employer's Statement confirming date last worked and job description
- [ ] Specialist reports, if applicable
- [ ] CPP disability decision letter, if available
- [ ] Tax returns (for income verification on own-occupation claims)

## 4. Approval Authority Levels

Claims must be approved by the appropriate authority level based on the claim amount and complexity:

| Authority Level | Claim Amount | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Up to $100,000 | Claims Adjuster (minimum 2 years experience) |
| Level 2 | $100,001 to $500,000 | Senior Claims Adjuster |
| Level 3 | $500,001 to $1,000,000 | Claims Team Lead |
| Level 4 | $1,000,001 to $5,000,000 | Claims Manager |
| Level 5 | Over $5,000,000 | Director of Claims, with AVP sign-off |

### 4.1 Dual Authorization

Claims exceeding $1,000,000 require dual authorization from two individuals at the appropriate authority level. Claims involving litigation, regulatory complaints, or media exposure require Claims Manager approval regardless of amount.

### 4.2 Denial Authority

All claim denials, regardless of claim amount, must be reviewed and approved by a Senior Claims Adjuster or higher. The denial letter must:
- Clearly state the reason(s) for denial with reference to specific policy provisions
- Inform the claimant of their right to appeal
- Provide information about the OmbudService for Life & Health Insurance (OLHI)
- Be reviewed by the legal department if the denial involves contestability or fraud

## 5. Fraud Indicators

### 5.1 Red Flags

Claims adjusters must be alert to the following fraud indicators and escalate to the Special Investigations Unit (SIU) when two or more indicators are present:

**Application Fraud Indicators:**
- Policy purchased within 2 years of the claim (contestability period)
- Large face amount relative to the policyholder's income or net worth
- Multiple policies taken out in a short period across different insurers
- Application completed by someone other than the applicant
- Inconsistencies between the application and medical records

**Death Claim Fraud Indicators:**
- Cause of death inconsistent with the insured's known medical history
- Death occurred in a foreign jurisdiction with limited verification capability
- Beneficiary changed shortly before the death
- Claimant is reluctant to provide documentation or delays submission
- Multiple claimants for the same policy
- Death certificate obtained from a jurisdiction different from where the death allegedly occurred

**Disability Claim Fraud Indicators:**
- Claimant's described limitations are inconsistent with surveillance or social media activity
- Treating physician is a known outlier in disability certification
- Claimant has a history of short-term disability claims
- Employer reports suspicion of malingering
- Claimant is self-employed with difficult-to-verify income

### 5.2 Escalation to Special Investigations Unit

When fraud is suspected, the adjuster must:
1. Document the specific indicators observed in the claim notes
2. Do not confront the claimant or indicate that fraud is suspected
3. Complete a Suspected Fraud Referral Form (Form SIU-RF-01)
4. Submit to the SIU within 2 business days
5. Continue normal claim processing unless directed otherwise by SIU

The SIU will conduct an investigation and provide a recommendation within 30 business days. The adjuster must not deny a claim solely based on suspicion; the SIU investigation must substantiate the fraud allegation.

## 6. Processing Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

### 6.1 Target Turnaround Times

| Activity | SLA |
|---|---|
| Initial acknowledgement of claim | 2 business days from notification |
| Claim forms sent to claimant | 2 business days from notification |
| Initial review of submitted documentation | 5 business days from receipt |
| Request for additional information | 5 business days from initial review |
| Straightforward claim decision | 15 business days from receipt of complete documentation |
| Complex claim decision | 30 business days from receipt of complete documentation |
| Payment processing after approval | 5 business days |
| Denial letter issuance | 5 business days from denial decision |

### 6.2 Definition of Complex Claims

A claim is classified as complex if any of the following apply:
- Death occurred within the contestability period
- Fraud indicators are present
- Multiple beneficiaries with conflicting claims
- Foreign death or disability
- Litigation or regulatory complaint is involved
- Cause of death or disability is disputed
- Claim amount exceeds $1,000,000
- Policy involves multiple riders with separate adjudication

### 6.3 SLA Monitoring

SLA compliance is monitored monthly by the Claims Operations team. Adjusters falling below 90% SLA compliance for two consecutive months will receive a performance improvement plan. The divisional target is 95% SLA compliance.

## 7. Escalation Procedures

### 7.1 Internal Escalation Path

| Level | Contact | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Claims Team Lead | Adjuster unable to reach a decision, SLA at risk |
| Level 2 | Claims Manager | Denial of claim over $500,000, regulatory complaint, media inquiry |
| Level 3 | Director of Claims | Litigation, OSFI inquiry, systemic issues |
| Level 4 | VP Claims Operations | Class action, regulatory enforcement, reputational risk |

### 7.2 External Escalation

Claimants who are dissatisfied with a claims decision may escalate through the following channels:
1. **Internal Appeal**: Written request to the Claims Manager within 90 days of the decision
2. **Manulife Ombudsman**: Independent internal review
3. **OmbudService for Life & Health Insurance (OLHI)**: External dispute resolution (1-888-295-8112)
4. **Financial regulators**: Applicable provincial insurance regulator or OSFI
5. **Legal proceedings**: Civil court action within the applicable limitation period

### 7.3 Regulatory Inquiries

All inquiries from OSFI, provincial insurance regulators, or the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC) must be immediately escalated to the Director of Claims and the Legal & Compliance department. Responses to regulatory inquiries must be coordinated through Legal & Compliance.

## 8. Documentation and Record Retention

### 8.1 Claim File Documentation

All claim files must contain:
- Chronological claim notes documenting every action taken, decision made, and communication with the claimant
- All correspondence (inbound and outbound)
- All medical and supporting documentation
- Decision rationale with specific policy provision references
- Approval or denial authorization records

### 8.2 Record Retention

Claim files must be retained in accordance with Manulife's Records Retention Policy:
- Paid claims: 7 years from the date of final payment
- Denied claims: 10 years from the date of denial
- Litigation files: 10 years from the date of final resolution
- Fraud investigation files: Permanently

### 8.3 Privacy and Confidentiality

All claim information is confidential and must be handled in accordance with PIPEDA and Manulife's Privacy Policy. Medical information must not be disclosed to unauthorized parties, including the policyholder's employer. Plan members have the right to access their personal information held in the claim file, subject to the exceptions in PIPEDA.

---

## 9. Quality Assurance

### 9.1 File Audits

The Quality Assurance team conducts monthly audits of a random sample of 10% of completed claim files. Audit criteria include:
- Accuracy of the claims decision
- Completeness of documentation
- Adherence to SLAs
- Compliance with CLHIA guidelines
- Appropriateness of communication with the claimant

### 9.2 Corrective Actions

Audit findings are communicated to the adjuster and their team lead within 5 business days. Material deficiencies require a corrective action plan. Systemic issues identified through audits are escalated to the Claims Manager for process improvement.
