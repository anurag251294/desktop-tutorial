# Investment Commentary — Q1 2026 (Manulife Japan)

## Market Summary

Q1 2026 saw the TOPIX advance 4.8% in JPY terms, driven by continued earnings momentum at large-cap exporters and a steady weakening of the yen against the US dollar (USDJPY closed Q1 near 158). Bank of Japan policy remained accommodative with no rate change at the March meeting; the next BoJ meeting is expected to debate ending negative-rate guidance in Q3.

## Asset Allocation View

- **Japanese equities**: Constructive. Corporate governance reforms are still translating into shareholder returns. Maintain overweight.
- **Japanese government bonds**: Underweight pending BoJ policy clarity. Duration risk asymmetric to the upside if policy normalises.
- **Asia ex-Japan**: Neutral. China property and US trade-tariff policy are key swing factors.
- **US equities**: Neutral after Q1 mega-cap rally; valuations stretched but earnings remain solid.
- **Yen money market**: Maintain only for short-horizon capital preservation.

## Outlook H2 2026

Risks: BoJ policy surprise, USDJPY volatility around any FX-intervention rhetoric, China growth slowdown, geopolitical tensions over Taiwan trade.

Opportunities: Japan corporate buybacks at record levels, ongoing Tokyo Stock Exchange Prime listing reforms, AI-related capex from Japanese semiconductor equipment makers, domestic wage-cycle continuing into the spring shunto wage round.
