# Customer Service FAQ — Manulife Japan

## How do I pay my premium?

You can pay your premium by:
- Direct debit (kouza furikae) from any Japanese bank account
- Bank transfer (furikomi)
- Convenience store (konbini) payment slip mailed to your address
- Credit card (some products only)

For monthly direct-debit set-up, please submit form MLJ-PMT-01 to your local branch.

## What happens if I miss a premium?

A 60-day grace period applies from the premium due date. During the grace period, the policy remains in force. After 60 days the policy lapses. Lapsed policies may be reinstated within 24 months subject to medical evidence and arrears with interest.

## How do I update my address?

Submit a change-of-address form at any branch or via the My Manulife Japan portal. Address changes within 90 days of a claim are flagged for AML review per the Claims Processing Guidelines.

## Can I make my policy paid up?

Whole life and endowment policies may be made paid-up after the third anniversary. The reduced sum insured will be calculated based on the cash value at the date of the request.

## How do I designate or change beneficiaries?

You may designate up to four primary beneficiaries. Changes require signed form MLJ-BNF-01 and a copy of your government-issued ID. Changes take effect on receipt at Manulife Japan's processing centre.

## What is the contestability period?

For life insurance products, two years (24 months) from the effective date. During this period the insurer can rescind the policy for material misstatement. After 24 months the policy becomes incontestable except for fraud.

## Where do I file a complaint?

You can:
1. Contact your advisor or branch office
2. Call the Customer Service Hotline: 0120-XXX-XXX (toll-free in Japan)
3. Submit through the My Manulife Japan portal
4. Escalate to the Japan Financial Services Agency consumer hotline if unresolved within 30 days

## Tax forms

Manulife Japan issues annual premium-paid certificates (hoken-ryou koujo shoumeisho) by mid-October each year. These are required for the personal income tax filing.
