# Advisor Handbook — Compliance & Conduct (Manulife Japan)

## 1. Licensing & Registration

All Manulife Japan advisors must hold:
- Insurance Solicitor registration with the Life Insurance Association of Japan (Seimei Hoken Kyoukai)
- For variable annuity sales: Type 2 Financial Instruments Business registration
- For sales to corporate group plans: Corporate Pension License

Re-certification is mandatory every 24 months. Continuing education credits are tracked in the My Manulife Japan advisor portal.

## 2. Suitability Framework

Before any product recommendation, the advisor must:
1. Complete the customer profile assessment (age, income, dependents, investment horizon, risk tolerance, knowledge level)
2. Verify the customer's tax residency and identify any cross-border considerations
3. Document the recommendation rationale with reference to at least two of the assessed factors
4. Obtain the customer's signed acknowledgement before policy issue

## 3. Conflict of Interest Disclosure

Commission and trail-commission rates must be disclosed in writing for any product sold. This includes:
- First-year commission as a percentage of annualised premium
- Trail commission rate and duration
- Any non-cash incentives such as marketing support or training events

## 4. Anti-Money Laundering Duties

Advisors must:
- Verify customer identity using government-issued ID at policy issue
- Re-verify identity for any policy alteration above JPY 5,000,000 single premium
- Report any of the following to the AML/Fraud Investigation Unit within 1 business day:
  - Cash payments above JPY 1,000,000
  - Customer reluctance to disclose source of funds
  - Multiple policy applications across short periods with inconsistent customer profiles
  - Beneficiary changes within 90 days of a known life event in the family

## 5. Sales to Vulnerable Customers

Additional safeguards apply when the customer is:
- Age 75 or above
- Recently bereaved within 12 months
- Reported by family members as having cognitive concerns

Sales to vulnerable customers require:
- Second advisor present at the consultation
- 7-day cooling-off period
- Branch manager sign-off on the final application

## 6. Complaint Handling

All complaints must be logged in the Complaints Management System within 1 business day. Failure to log is itself a conduct breach. Complaints relating to suitability are escalated to the Compliance Office; complaints relating to claims are routed to the Claims Director.

## 7. Sanctions

| Breach | First incident | Repeat |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Late complaint logging | Written warning | Probation |
| Suitability documentation gap | Re-training + branch manager review | Termination |
| Undisclosed conflict of interest | Termination | n/a |
| AML failure to report | Termination + regulatory reporting | n/a |
